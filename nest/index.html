species

although has been once observed making iconic "dee dee dee" sounds, his markings look more that of a chestnut chickadee. meanwhile, his body size and beak shape are far more corvid-like. we don't know if this is a corvid pretending to be a songbird, or something else entirely. he seems to be happiest identifying with the "dee" name.

diet

after having an unpleasant awakening to frat boy culture and college kid cuisine, he takes it upon himself to make nutty berry smoothies with various powders and vegetables in them. he's also a fan of kombucha, wine, and essentially anything fruity and fermented. always has a mild moral quandry about eating bugs, even though other birds of his type do so without hesitation.

song

this bird has the lucky gift of obtaining sentience, and has adapted english as a primary spoken language. he isn't able to make the same kind of calls that his ancestors once could. but he likes to rhyme and make tongue-in-cheek wordplay out of the english language to fulfil the joys most songbirds get from reproducing song.
